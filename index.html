I am Shicheng Guo, Ph.D., a statistical and computational geneticist and Senior Director of Translational Genetics & Data Science at Arrowhead Pharmaceuticals, working at the convergence of human genetics, artificial intelligence, real-world evidence (RWE), and population-scale biobanks to redefine how medicines are discovered and developed; over more than a decade — spanning Fudan University, UT Health Houston, UC San Diego, and the Marshfield Clinic & University of Wisconsin–Madison — I have built the methods and platforms that turn genomes, electronic health records, and multi-omic data into validated drug targets and biomarkers, from a gene-based recessive-diplotype scan that uncovered FGF6 in iron metabolism (Blood) to methylation-based liquid-biopsy approaches for non-invasive, tissue-of-origin detection (Nature Genetics); and today my focus is the future of pharmaceutical R&D in the AI age — integrating biobank-scale human genetics and longitudinal real-world data with machine learning and foundation models to prioritize targets backed by causal human evidence, stratify patients, de-risk programs earlier, and accelerate RNA-based and precision therapeutics, building the data foundations and the teams that let AI move drug discovery from intuition to evidence, and from population averages to the right medicine for the right patient.

Focus Areas

AI & Machine Learning

Foundation models and ML over multi-omic and clinical data to prioritize targets and de-risk programs in the AI age of pharma.

Real-World Evidence

Electronic health records and longitudinal real-world data linked to genetics for target validation and patient stratification.

Biobank-Scale Genetics

Human-genetics-driven target discovery and validation across UK Biobank, eMERGE, PMRP, and other population-scale cohorts.

Epigenetics & Liquid Biopsy

DNA methylation, methylation haplotype blocks, and cell-free DNA for non-invasive detection and biomarkers.

Highlights

  • Introduced a novel gene-based recessive diplotype approach that discovered FGF6 as a new iron-metabolism gene — published in Blood (2019).
  • Contributed to tissue-of-origin mapping from cell-free DNA methylation — Nature Genetics (2017).
  • Co-authored foundational methylome studies in Nature Biotechnology and PLoS Biology.
