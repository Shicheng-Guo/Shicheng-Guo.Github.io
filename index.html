I build the computational and human-genetic evidence that decides which targets become drugs — and which patients they can help.

I am a statistical and computational geneticist with over a decade at the intersection of genomics, epigenetics, and machine learning. As Senior Director of Translational Genetics & Data Science at Arrowhead Pharmaceuticals, I lead human-genetics-driven target discovery and biomarker strategy for RNA-based therapeutics — connecting population-scale data, functional genomics, and clinical insight to advance precision medicine from the bench to the clinic.

Before industry, I trained and worked across leading academic and clinical research institutions — Fudan University (Ph.D., 2014), UT Health Houston, UC San Diego, and the Marshfield Clinic Research Institute & University of Wisconsin–Madison — contributing to landmark studies in DNA methylation, cell-free DNA, and rare-variant disease genetics.

Research Focus

Translational Genetics

Human-genetics-driven target identification and validation across the druggable genome.

Epigenetics & Methylomics

Methylation, cell-free DNA, and aging-clock biomarkers for diagnosis and prognosis.

Statistical Genetics

GWAS, rare-variant and gene-based methods across population-scale biobanks.

Data Science & AI/ML

Scalable pipelines and predictive models over multi-omic and clinical big data.

Highlights

  • Introduced a novel gene-based recessive diplotype approach that discovered FGF6 as a new iron-metabolism gene — published in Blood (2019).
  • Contributed to tissue-of-origin mapping from cell-free DNA methylation — Nature Genetics (2017).
  • Co-authored foundational methylome studies in Nature Biotechnology and PLoS Biology.
