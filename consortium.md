<p class="lead">Modern human genetics is a team sport. No single cohort is large enough to resolve rare-variant effects or rare diseases, so the field organizes into consortia — harmonizing phenotypes, sharing summary statistics, and meta-analyzing across millions of participants. Below is a curated map of the consortia that matter most for statistical genetics, target identification, and translational drug discovery.</p>

<h2 class="archive__subtitle">Rare-Variant & Sequencing Consortia</h2>

<div class="portfolio-grid">
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://brava-genetics.github.io/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-dna"></i>
    <h3>BRaVa</h3>
    <p>The <strong>Biobank Rare Variant Analysis</strong> consortium runs harmonized, gene-based rare-variant association tests across global biobanks. Using a shared phenotype dictionary and a common SAIGE-GENE+ pipeline, BRaVa meta-analyzes exome/genome sequencing to find rare coding variants driving disease — the resolution single biobanks lack.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Rare variants</span><span class="tag">Exome</span><span class="tag">Meta-analysis</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://gnomad.broadinstitute.org/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-database"></i>
    <h3>gnomAD</h3>
    <p>The <strong>Genome Aggregation Database</strong> (successor to ExAC) is the field's reference for allele frequencies, aggregating exomes and genomes from hundreds of thousands of individuals across diverse populations. Essential for variant interpretation, constraint metrics (pLI, LOEUF), and filtering candidate disease variants.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Allele frequency</span><span class="tag">Constraint</span><span class="tag">Broad</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://topmed.nhlbi.nih.gov/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-heart-pulse"></i>
    <h3>TOPMed</h3>
    <p>NHLBI's <strong>Trans-Omics for Precision Medicine</strong> program generates deep whole-genome sequencing across diverse cohorts, powering rare-variant discovery for heart, lung, blood, and sleep traits — and underpinning one of the most widely used imputation reference panels.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">WGS</span><span class="tag">NHLBI</span><span class="tag">Imputation</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://genebass.org/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-magnifying-glass-chart"></i>
    <h3>Genebass</h3>
    <p>A browsable resource of gene-based and single-variant association results from UK Biobank exome sequencing (~400k participants, thousands of phenotypes). A practical entry point for exploring exome-wide rare-variant signals without re-running analyses.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">UK Biobank</span><span class="tag">Exome</span><span class="tag">PheWAS</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://rgc-research.regeneron.com/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-flask-vial"></i>
    <h3>Regeneron Genetics Center</h3>
    <p>Through the DiscovEHR collaboration and large-scale exome sequencing of biobank cohorts, the RGC links genetics to electronic health records at industrial scale — a model for genetics-first drug-target discovery in pharma.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Exome</span><span class="tag">EHR</span><span class="tag">Target ID</span></div>
  </a>
</div>

<h2 class="archive__subtitle">Biobank Networks & Meta-Analysis</h2>

<div class="portfolio-grid">
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://www.globalbiobankmeta.org/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-globe"></i>
    <h3>GBMI</h3>
    <p>The <strong>Global Biobank Meta-analysis Initiative</strong> is a collaborative network of biobanks spanning multiple continents and ancestries. It develops harmonized phenotype definitions and meta-analyzes GWAS across cohorts to improve power and trans-ancestry generalizability — the backbone many other efforts (including BRaVa) build on.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Biobanks</span><span class="tag">Trans-ancestry</span><span class="tag">GWAS</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://www.finngen.fi/en">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-snowflake"></i>
    <h3>FinnGen</h3>
    <p>A public–private partnership combining genome data with national health registries for hundreds of thousands of Finnish participants. Finland's population history enriches otherwise-rare deleterious variants, making FinnGen exceptionally powerful for loss-of-function discovery.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Finland</span><span class="tag">Registry</span><span class="tag">LoF</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://www.mvp.va.gov/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-shield-halved"></i>
    <h3>Million Veteran Program</h3>
    <p>The U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs' MVP is one of the world's largest genomic cohorts linked to longitudinal EHR, with strong representation of African- and Hispanic-ancestry participants — critical for equitable, multi-ancestry genetic discovery.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">VA</span><span class="tag">Multi-ancestry</span><span class="tag">EHR</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://allofus.nih.gov/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-users"></i>
    <h3>All of Us</h3>
    <p>NIH's flagship precision-medicine program is enrolling a million-plus diverse U.S. participants with whole-genome sequencing, EHR, surveys, and wearables — deliberately oversampling communities historically underrepresented in research.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">NIH</span><span class="tag">WGS</span><span class="tag">Diversity</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://www.genomicsengland.co.uk/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-hospital"></i>
    <h3>Genomics England</h3>
    <p>Born from the 100,000 Genomes Project, Genomics England embeds whole-genome sequencing into the NHS for rare disease and cancer, pairing clinical-grade interpretation with a research environment for discovery.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">NHS</span><span class="tag">Rare disease</span><span class="tag">Cancer</span></div>
  </a>
</div>

<h2 class="archive__subtitle">GWAS Aggregation, Catalogs & Target ID</h2>

<div class="portfolio-grid">
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://www.ebi.ac.uk/gwas/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-book-open"></i>
    <h3>NHGRI-EBI GWAS Catalog</h3>
    <p>The authoritative, manually curated catalog of published genome-wide association studies and their summary statistics. The starting point for any locus lookup, polygenic score, or downstream functional follow-up.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Catalog</span><span class="tag">Summary stats</span><span class="tag">EBI</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://www.opentargets.org/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-bullseye"></i>
    <h3>Open Targets</h3>
    <p>A partnership of EMBL-EBI, the Wellcome Sanger Institute, and pharma that integrates genetics, genomics, and chemistry to systematically score and prioritize drug targets — operationalizing the genetics-to-therapeutics pipeline.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Target ID</span><span class="tag">Drug discovery</span><span class="tag">Integration</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://www.icda.bio/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-network-wired"></i>
    <h3>ICDA</h3>
    <p>The <strong>International Common Disease Alliance</strong> coordinates the global community translating genetic associations for common disease into mechanism and therapeutics, setting shared roadmaps and standards across cohorts and methods.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Common disease</span><span class="tag">Roadmap</span><span class="tag">Mechanism</span></div>
  </a>
</div>

<h2 class="archive__subtitle">Trait- & Disease-Specific Consortia</h2>

<div class="portfolio-grid">
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://pgc.unc.edu/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-brain"></i>
    <h3>Psychiatric Genomics Consortium</h3>
    <p>The PGC is the largest collaboration in the history of psychiatry, meta-analyzing GWAS for schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, depression, ADHD, autism, and more — a template for how to scale genetics in complex, heterogeneous traits.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Psychiatry</span><span class="tag">GWAS</span><span class="tag">Meta-analysis</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://portals.broadinstitute.org/collaboration/giant/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-ruler-vertical"></i>
    <h3>GIANT</h3>
    <p>The <strong>Genetic Investigation of ANthropometric Traits</strong> consortium defined the genetic architecture of height, BMI, and body shape across millions of individuals — landmark work on polygenicity and effect-size distributions.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Anthropometric</span><span class="tag">Height/BMI</span><span class="tag">Polygenic</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://www.chargeconsortium.com/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-heart"></i>
    <h3>CHARGE</h3>
    <p><strong>Cohorts for Heart and Aging Research in Genomic Epidemiology</strong> unites prospective cohorts to study cardiovascular, metabolic, and aging-related phenotypes, pioneering large-scale GWAS meta-analysis design.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Cardiovascular</span><span class="tag">Aging</span><span class="tag">Cohorts</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://enigma.ini.usc.edu/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-head-side-virus"></i>
    <h3>ENIGMA</h3>
    <p><strong>Enhancing NeuroImaging Genetics through Meta-Analysis</strong> links brain imaging phenotypes to genetics across hundreds of institutions, mapping the genetic basis of brain structure and neuropsychiatric disease.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Neuroimaging</span><span class="tag">Brain</span><span class="tag">Genetics</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://diagram-consortium.org/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-droplet"></i>
    <h3>DIAGRAM / DIAMANTE</h3>
    <p>The diabetes genetics consortia (DIAGRAM and its trans-ancestry successor DIAMANTE) have driven type 2 diabetes locus discovery and fine-mapping across global populations, a model for ancestry-diverse GWAS.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Type 2 diabetes</span><span class="tag">Fine-mapping</span><span class="tag">Trans-ancestry</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://lipidgenetics.org/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-vials"></i>
    <h3>Global Lipids Genetics Consortium</h3>
    <p>The GLGC meta-analyzes blood-lipid GWAS (LDL, HDL, triglycerides, total cholesterol) across millions of participants — work that has directly informed cardiovascular drug targets such as PCSK9.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Lipids</span><span class="tag">Cardiovascular</span><span class="tag">PCSK9</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://ckdgen.imbi.uni-freiburg.de/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-disease"></i>
    <h3>CKDGen</h3>
    <p>The <strong>Chronic Kidney Disease Genetics</strong> consortium meta-analyzes GWAS for kidney function (eGFR), CKD, and related biomarkers across global cohorts, mapping the genetic architecture of renal disease.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Kidney</span><span class="tag">eGFR</span><span class="tag">GWAS</span></div>
  </a>
</div>

<h2 class="archive__subtitle">Functional Genomics & Reference Resources</h2>

<div class="portfolio-grid">
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://www.internationalgenome.org/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-earth-americas"></i>
    <h3>1000 Genomes / IGSR</h3>
    <p>The 1000 Genomes Project, now sustained by the International Genome Sample Resource, produced the first deep catalog of human variation across populations — still a workhorse reference and imputation panel.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Reference</span><span class="tag">Variation</span><span class="tag">Populations</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://gtexportal.org/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-diagram-project"></i>
    <h3>GTEx</h3>
    <p>The <strong>Genotype-Tissue Expression</strong> project maps eQTLs and sQTLs across dozens of human tissues, providing the regulatory bridge that connects GWAS loci to genes and mechanisms.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">eQTL</span><span class="tag">Expression</span><span class="tag">Tissues</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://www.encodeproject.org/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-layer-group"></i>
    <h3>ENCODE</h3>
    <p>The <strong>Encyclopedia of DNA Elements</strong> annotates functional elements — regulatory regions, chromatin states, transcription-factor binding — across cell types, essential for interpreting non-coding variants.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Regulatory</span><span class="tag">Epigenomics</span><span class="tag">Annotation</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://clinicalgenome.org/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-stethoscope"></i>
    <h3>ClinGen & ClinVar</h3>
    <p>The NIH Clinical Genome Resource (ClinGen) and the ClinVar archive define and aggregate clinical variant interpretations — the shared standard for gene–disease validity and variant pathogenicity in clinical genetics.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Clinical</span><span class="tag">Pathogenicity</span><span class="tag">Curation</span></div>
  </a>
  <a class="portfolio-card" href="https://www.humancellatlas.org/">
    <i class="fa-solid fa-microscope"></i>
    <h3>Human Cell Atlas</h3>
    <p>An international effort to map every human cell type at single-cell resolution, building the reference that increasingly grounds where and how disease genes act.</p>
    <div class="tags"><span class="tag">Single-cell</span><span class="tag">Atlas</span><span class="tag">Reference</span></div>
  </a>
</div>

<p style="margin-top:2.4em;color:var(--muted);font-size:.9rem">
  This is a curated, non-exhaustive guide focused on consortia most relevant to statistical genetics and translational drug discovery. Membership, scale, and scope evolve continually — follow each consortium's site for current data releases and participation details.
</p>
